Tracing of Cartesian Curves

Q1. \({y^2}={x^3\over (2a-x)}\)

1. Symmetry:

As Power of y is even. So, we can say that curve is symmetrical about X-axis.

2. Point of intersection

a) Origin:

If we put x=0 in equation we get \(y=0\). So Curve is passing through origin.

b) Tangent at origin: \({y^2}={x^3\over (2a-x)}\)
⸫ \({2ay^2-xy^2}={x^3}\)
So, lowest degree term is \(2ay^2\)
Hence by Newton’s Law \(2ay^2=0\) => \(y^2=0\) => y=0 … (is tangent at origin)

c) X-intercept
If we put y=0 in given Equation We get X-intercept.
⸫ \(0={x^3\over(2a-x)}\)
⸫ \(x^3=0\)
⸫ \(x=0\) … (is the X-intercept)

d) Y-intercept
If we put x=0 in given Equation We get Y-intercept.
⸫ \(y^2=0\)
⸫ \(y=0\) … (is the Y-intercept)

⸫ Curve Passing through (0,0).

3.Asymptotes

a) Parallel to X-axis
Our equation is \(y^2=x^3/(2a-x)\)
i.e. \(2ay^2-xy^2-x^3=0\)
Here, highest power of x is 3.
For Asymptote parallel to x-axis we equate coefficient of highest power equals zero. But here coefficient of \(x^3\) is -1.
So, no asymptote is parallel to X-axis.

b) Parallel to Y-axis
Here, highest power of y is 2.
For Asymptote parallel to Y-axis we equate coefficient of highest power equals zero.
⸫ 2a-x=0
⸫ x=2a … (asymptote parallel to Y-axis)

4.Special Points

For special Points we need \({ⅆy\overⅆx}\)

⸫ \(y^2=x^3/(2a-x) \)

⸫ \(2y {ⅆy\overⅆx}={{([2a-x](3x^2 )-(x^3 )(-1))}\over(2a-x)^2}\)

⸫ \( {ⅆy\overⅆx}={{(6ax^2-3x^3+x^3)}\over(2[2a-x]^2 y)}\)

⸫ \( {ⅆy\overⅆx}={(6ax^2-2x^3)\over(2(2a-x)^2 y)}\)

⸫\( {ⅆy\overⅆx}={(x^2 (3a-x))\over((2a-x)^2 y)}\)

Case-1) for \(x=2a\) >>> \({ⅆy\overⅆx}= ∞\)
⸫We can say
At \((2a,∞)\) >>> Tangent is parallel to Y-axis. i.e. \(x=2a\) is Tangent at \(y = ∞\). ... Which is Asymptote.
Case-2) for \(y=0\) >>> \({ⅆy\overⅆx}= ∞\)
⸫We can say
At (0,0) Tangent is parallel to Y-axis
i.e. x=0 is tangent at origin.

5.Region of Absence

We have \({y^2}={x^3\over (2a-x)}\)
i.e. \(y=√{{x^3}\over⟨2a-x⟩}\)
a) For \(x>2a\), y becomes imaginary
Hence for \(x>2a\) curve not exists.
b) For \(x<0 \), y becomes imaginary
Hence for \(x<0 \) curve not exists.
