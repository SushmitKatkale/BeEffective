Co-ordinate System

Q1. Find the projection of the join of point (1, 2, 1) and (-1, 0, 2) on the line joining the points (0, 1, 3), (5, 0, 2).

Let, the points be \( A≡(1,2,-1),B≡(-1,0,2) \)
\(P≡(0,1,3) \) and \(Q≡(5,0,2)\)
We have to find the projection of \(\vec{AB}\) on line PQ.
let, θ be the angle between \(\vec{AB}\) ̅and line PQ.
\(\vec{PQ} = \vec{q}-\vec{p} = 5\vec{i}-\vec{j}-\vec{k}\)
\(\vec{AB}\) = \( \vec{b}-\vec{a} \) = \( -2\vec{i}-2\vec{j}+\vec{k}\)
\( |\vec {AB}|=√(4+4+1)=√9=3 \)
\( |\vec {PQ}|=√(25+1+1) = √27=3√3 \)
\( cos⁡θ={(-10+2-1)\over (3)(3√3)} \)
\( cosθ={-9\over 9√3}={1\over -√3}\)
⸫ Projection of (AB) ̅ on line PQ =\(|\vec {AB}| cos⁡θ\)
=\( (3){-1\over √3} \)
⸫ Projection of \( \vec {AB}\) on line PQ \( =-√3 \)

Q2. Show that the triangle whose vertices are the points (1, 4, 2), (-2, 1, 2), (2,-3, 4) is a right angled triangle. Find also the other angles.

Let the points be \( A≡(1,4,2),B≡(-2,1,2)\) and
\( C≡(2,-3,4) \)
We have to find the \( ∠A ,∠B ,∠C \)
Dr’s for \( \vec{AB} ≡ -3:-3:0\)
Dr’s for \( \vec{BC} ≡ 4:-4:2 \)
Dr’s for \( \vec{AC} ≡ 1:-7:2 \)
⸫ \( cos⁡B={(-12+12+0)\over (√(9+9) √(16+16+4)} \)
⸫ \( cos⁡B=0≫∠B=90^0 \) … (Hence Proved)
Also, \( cos⁡A={(-3+21+0)\over √(9+9) √(1+49+4)} \)
\( ∠A=cos^{-1}({18\over (3√2)[3√6]} ) \)
\( ∠A=cos^{-1} ({1\over √3})=54.73^0 \)
We know \( ∠A+ ∠B+∠C=180 \)
\( ∠C=180-(90+54.73)=35.26^0 \)
⸫ Angles of triangle are \( ∠B=90^0,∠A=54.73^0 \) and
\( ∠C=35.26^0 \)

Q3. A line makes angles α,β,γ and δ with four diagonals of a cube. Show that
\( cos^2⁡α\)+\(cos^2⁡β\)+\(cos^2⁡γ\)+\(cos^2⁡δ\)=\({4\over 3} \)

Let Origin is one of the edge of cube as shown in figure. ⸫ \( O(0,0,0),A(a,0,0),B(a,a,0),C(0,a,0) \)
⸫ \( P(a,a,a),Q(0,a,a),R(0,0,a),S(a,0,a) \)
⸫ OP, AQ, RB, SC are the diagonals of cube
Hence
Dr’s of OP \( ≡a:a:a≡1:1:1\)
Dr’s of AQ \( ≡-a:a:a≡-1:1:1\)
Dr’s of \(RB ≡a:a:-a≡1:1:-1\)
Dr’s of SC \(≡a:-a:a≡1:-1:1\)
Hence Direction cosines of diagonals are
Dc’s of OP \(≡{1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\)
Dc’s of AQ \(≡{-1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\)
Dc’s of RB\(≡{1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\):\({-1\over √3}\)
Dc’s of SC \(≡{1\over √3}\):\({-1\over √3}\):\({1\over √3}\)
Let direction cosines of line is \( l:m:n \)
Line makes and of α,β,γ and δ with these diagonals
⸫ \( cos⁡α={1\over √3}+{1\over √3}+{1\over √3} \)
⸫\( cos⁡β=-{1\over √3}+{1\over √3}+{1\over √3} \)
⸫\(cos⁡γ={1\over √3}+{1\over √3}-{1\over √3} \)
⸫\( cos⁡δ={1\over √3}-{1\over √3}+{1\over √3}\)
⸫as per show that
\( cos^2⁡α+cos^2⁡β+cos^2⁡γ+cos^2⁡δ \)
\( =({1\over √3}+{1\over √3}+{1\over √3})^2\) +\((-{1\over √3}+{1\over √3}+{1\over √3})^2 \)
\(+({1\over √3}+{1\over √3}-{1\over √3})^2\) +\(({1\over √3}:{-1\over √3}:{1\over √3})^2 \)
\(= {4\over 3} (l^2+m^2+n^2 ) \)
\(= {4\over 3} (1)\) … (Hence Proved)

Q4. Find the ratio in which the line joining the points (2, 4, 5), (3, 5, -4) is divided by the XY-plane.

Let \( A≡(2,4,5), B≡(3,5,-4) \)
\( C≡(a,b,0) \) …(as c is in XY-Plane)
Let, C divides AB in ration \( m: n \)
So, \( 0={(5n+(-4)m)\over {(m+n)}}\)
⸫ \( 5n-4m=σ \)
⸫\( 5n=4m \)
⸫\( {m\over n}={5\over 4 }\)
Hence, C divides AB in ratio \( 5:4\).

Q5. Prove that the two lines whose direction cosines are connected by the two relations \( al+bm+cn=0 \) And \( ul^2+vm^2+wn^2=0 \) Are
perpendicular if \( a^2 (ν+w)\) + \( b^2 (w+u)\) +\(c^2 (u+v)=0 \)
And parallel if \( {a^2\over u} \) + \( {b^2\over v} \) + \({c^2\over w}=0 \)

Let, \( al+bm+cn=0 \) …(1)
And \( ul^2+vm^2+wn^2=0 \) … (2)
⸫ \(l=-{(bm+cn)\over a} \) put in equation (2)
⸫ \( ul^2+vm^2+wn^2=0 \)
⸫ \( u-{(bm+cn)\over a)}^2+vm^2+wn^2=0 \)
⸫ \( u(bm+cn)^2+m^2 a^2 v\)+\(wa^2 n^2=0 \)
⸫ \( u(b^2 m^2+c^2 n^2+2cbmn)\) + \( m^2 a^2 v\) + \(wa^2 n^2=0\)
⸫ \( (ub^2+va^2)m^2 \) + \( 2ucbmn \) +\((uc^2+wa^2)n^2=0 \)
⸫ \( (ub^2+va^2)({m\over n})^2\)+\(2ucb {m\over n}\)+\((uc^2+wa^2)=0 \) … (3)
This is the quadratic equation in \( m\over n\)
So, product of roots is c/a
So, \( {uc^2+wa^2\over ub^2+va^2}={m_1 m_2\over n_1 n_2 }\)
⸫ n_1 n_2/ub^2+va^2=m_1 m_2/uc^2+wa^2
Similarly we can say
\({n_1 n_2\over ub^2+va^2}={m_1 m_2\over uc^2+wa^2}={l_1 l_2\over ub^2+va^2}=K\)
Condition for perpendicular lines is
\(l_1 l_2+m_1 m_2+n_1 n_2=0\)
⸫ \( K(ub^2+va^2 )\) + \( K(uc^2+wa^2 ) \) +\( K(ub^2+va^2 )=0\)
⸫ \( a^2 (ν+w)+b^2 (w+u)+c^2 (u+v)=0 \) … (Hence Proved)

From equation (4)
For parallel line \(Δ=0\)
⸫\(b^2-4ac=0\)
⸫\((2ucb)^2-4(ub^2+va^2)(uc^2+wa^2)=0\)
⸫\( 4u^2 c^2 b^2\) - \(4(u^2 c^2 b^2+uwa^2 b^2+uva^2 c^2+vwa^4)=0\)
⸫ \( wua^2 b^2+uva^2 c^2+vwa^4=0 \)
Divide each term by \( a^2\over uvw \)
⸫ \({b^2\over v} \) + \( {c^2\over w} \) + \( {a^2\over u}=0\) …(Hence Proved)

Q6. Find the equation of the plane through the points (0, 1, 1), (2, 1, 3), (1,-2,-2).

Let points on plane are
\( A(0,1,1),B(2,1,3),C(1,-2,-2) \)
⸫ \(\vec{AB} = 2\vec{i} + 2 \vec{k}\) And \(\vec{BC} = -\vec{i} - 3 \vec{j}- 5 \vec{k}\)
̂ ⸫ \(\vec{n} = \vec{AB} ×\vec{BC}\))
\(\vec{n} \) is vector perpendicular to plane
⸫ \(\vec{n}= \begin{vmatrix} \vec{i} & \vec{j} & \vec{k} \\ 2 & 0 & 2 \\ -1 & -3 & -5\\ \end{vmatrix}\)

⸫\(\vec{n} = (0+6)\vec{i} - (-10+2) \vec{j}- 6 \vec{k}\)
⸫\(\vec{n} = 6\vec{i} +8 \vec{j}- 6 \vec{k}\) is vector perpendicular to plane
⸫Dr’s of \(\vec{n} \) are \( 3:4:-3\)
Let \( P(x, y, z) \) is the point on plane
⸫ Dr’s of \(\vec{AP} \) are \( (x):(y-1):(z-1) \)
And \(\vec{AP} ⊥ \vec{n}\)
So, \( x(3)+(y-1)(4)+(z-1)(-3)=0 \)
⸫ \( 3x+4y-3z-4+3=0\)
⸫ \( 3x+4y-3z-1=0 \) … (is the equation of plane)

Q7. Find the equation of the plane passing through the line of intersection of the planes \( x-2y+4z=5 \) , \(2x+y-4z-3=0 \) and perpendicular to plane \( 3x-4y+5z-5=0 \).

Let the planes are \( u≡ x-2y+4z-5 \)
\(v≡ 2x+y-4z-3 \)
\( w≡3x-4y+5z-5 \)
Line is passing through plane u and v
So, \( u+kv=0 \)
⸫ \( (x-2y+4z-5)\)+\(k(2x+y-4z-3)=0\)
⸫ \( (1+2k)x\) + \((-2+k)y\) +\( (4-4k)z\) +\( (-5-3k)=0\) … (1)
So, Dr’s of perpendicular vector are
\( (1+2k):(-2+k):(4-4k)\)
This Plane is perpendicular to plane w
Dr’s of perpendicular vector of w are \( 3:-4:5.\)
These planes are perpendicular
So, \( (1+2k)(3)\) + \( (-2+k)(-4)\) +\( (4-4k)(5)=0 \)
⸫ \( 3+6k+8-4k+20-20k=0 \)
⸫ \( 18k=31 \)
⸫ \( k={31\over 18} \)
⸫ Equation of plane is
\( (1+2{31\over 18})x \) + \( (-2+{31\over 18})y \) + \((4-4{31\over 18})z\) + \( (-5-3{31\over 18})=0 \)
⸫ \(80x-5y-50z-183=0 \) … (Is the required equation)

Q8. Find the condition that lines with dc’s \( (l_1,m_1,n_1) \) ,\( (l_2,m_2,n_2)\), \( (l_3,m_3,n_3) \) are coplanar.

We know that condition for three vectors to be coplanar is
\( \begin{vmatrix} a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \\ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \\ c_1 & c_2 & c_3 \\ \end{vmatrix} =0\)
So here Dc’s of parallel vectors of lines are given so Condition is
\( \begin{vmatrix} l_1 & l_2 & l_3 \\ m_1 & m_2 & m_3 \\ n_1 & n_2 & n_3 \\ \end{vmatrix} =0\)
