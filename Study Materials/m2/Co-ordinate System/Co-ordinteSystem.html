Co-ordinate System

Q1. Find the spherical co-ordinates of the points
i) (3,4,5)
ii) (-1,1,-1)

i) Here x=3,y=4,z=5
We know that \(r=√(x^2+y^2+z^2 )\)
⸫ \(r=√(9+16+25)\)
⸫ \(r=5√2 \)
Also we know that \(z=r cos⁡θ\)
⸫ \(cos⁡θ={z\over r} \)
⸫\(θ=cos^{-1}⁡({5\over(5√2)}) \)
⸫\(θ=45^0\)
Again we know that \(tan⁡ϕ={y\over x} \)
Here (3, 4, 5), x is positive and y is positive
So,\( ϕ=tan^{-1}({⁡4\over 3})\)
⸫ \(ϕ=53.13° \)
Answer: \( (3,4,5)≡(5√2,45^0,53.13°) \)

ii) Here x=-1,y=1,z=-1
We know that \(r=√(x^2+y^2+z^2 )\)
\( ⸫ r=√(1+1+1)\)
\( ⸫ r=√3\)
Also we know that z=r cos⁡θ
⸫ \(cos⁡θ={z\over r}\)
⸫\(θ=cos^{-1}⁡({(-1)\over √3})\)
⸫\(θ=180^0-cos^(-1)⁡(1/√3)\)
⸫\(θ=125.26^0 \)
Again we know that \(tan⁡ϕ={y\over x}≫ tan^{-1}⁡({1\over (-1)})\)
Here (-1, 1, -1), x is negative and y is positive
So,\( ϕ=180^0-tan^{-1}(1)\)
⸫\( ϕ=135^0\)
Answer: \((-1,1,-1)≡(√3,125.26^0,135^0)\)

Q2. Find the spherical co-ordinates of the points
i) (-2,1,-2)
ii) (-1,-2,-3)

i) Here x=-2,y=1,z=-2
We know that \(r=√(x^2+y^2+z^2 )\)
⸫ \(r=√(4+1+4)\)
⸫ \(r=3 \)
Also we know that \(z=r cos⁡θ\)
⸫ \(cos⁡θ={z\over r} \)
⸫\(θ=cos^{-1}⁡({-2\over 3}) \)
⸫\(θ=131.81^0\)
Again we know that \(tan⁡ϕ={y\over x} \)
Here (-2, 1, -2), x is negative and y is positive
So,\( ϕ=tan^{-1}({⁡1\over -2})\)
So,\( ϕ=180^0-tan^{-1}⁡({1/2})\)
⸫ \(ϕ=153.43° \)
Answer: \( (-2,1,-2)≡(3,131.81^0,153.43°) \)

ii) Here x=-1,y=-2,z=-3
We know that \(r=√(x^2+y^2+z^2 )\)
\( ⸫ r=√(1+4+9)\)
\( ⸫ r=√14\)
Also we know that z=r cos⁡θ
⸫ \(cos⁡θ={z\over r}\)
⸫\(θ=cos^{-1}⁡({(-3)\over √14})\)
⸫\(θ=180^0-cos^(-1)⁡(3/√14)\)
⸫\(θ=143.30^0 \)
Again we know that \(tan⁡ϕ={y\over x}≫ tan^{-1}⁡({2\over 1})\)
Here (-1, 1, -1), x is negative and y is negative
So,\( ϕ=180^0+tan^{-1}(2)\)
⸫\( ϕ=243.43^0\)
Answer: \((-1,-2,-3)≡(√14,143.30^0,243.43^0)\)

Q3. Find the cylindrical co-ordinates of the point, whose Cartesian co-ordinates are (-3, 4, 12).

Here x=-3,y=4,z=12
We know that \(ρ=√(x^2+y^2 ) \)
⸫\( ρ=√(9+16)\)
⸫\(ρ=5 \)
Also, we know that\( tan⁡ϕ={y\over x}≫ tan^{-1}⁡({4\over -3}) \)
As (-3,4, 12), x is negative and y is positive
So, \( ϕ=180^0-tan^{-1}⁡({4\over 3}) \)
⸫ \(ϕ=126.87^0\)
And z=12
Answer: \( (-3,4,12)≡(5,126.87^0,12)\)

Q4. Find the Cartesian co-ordinates of the point, \( (2,{5π\over 6},{3π\over 4})\).

Here \( r=2,θ={5π\over 6},ϕ={3π\over 4} \)
We know that \(x=r sin⁡θ cos⁡ϕ \)
⸫\( x=2 sin⁡({5π\over 6}) cos⁡({3π\over 4})\)
⸫ \(x={-1\over √2}\)
Also, we know that \(x=r sin⁡θ sin⁡ϕ \)
⸫\() y=2 sin({5π\over 6}) sin⁡({3π\over 4})\)
⸫ \(y={1\over √2}\)
Again, we know that y=r cos⁡θ
⸫ \(z=2 cos(⁡{5π\over 6}) \)
⸫ z=-√3
Answer: \((2,{5π\over 6},{3π\over 4})≡({-1\over √2},{1\over √2},-√3)\)

Q5 Find i) polar ii) cylindrical equation of surfaces whose Cartesian equation are
a) \(x^2+y^2=a^2\)
b) \(x^2+y^2+z^2=16\)

a) \(x^2+y^2=a^2\)
For polar equation put \(x=r cos⁡θ\) and \( y=r sin⁡θ \)
⸫\( r^2 cos^2⁡θ+r^2 sin^2⁡θ=a^2\)
⸫\(r=a\) …(is polar form)
For cylindrical equation put \(x=ρ cos⁡ϕ \)and \(y=sinϕ \)
⸫ \(ρ^2 cos^2⁡ϕ+ρ^2 sin^2⁡ϕ=a^2\)
⸫\(ρ=a\) …(is cylindrical form)

b) \(x^2+y^2+z^2=14\)
For polar equation put\(x=r sin⁡θ cos⁡ϕ \),
\(y=r sin⁡θ sin⁡ϕ\) And \(z=cos⁡θ \)
⸫ \(r^2 [sin^2 θ(cos^2⁡ϕ+sin^2⁡ϕ )+cos^2⁡θ ]=4^2\)
⸫\(r=4\) …(is polar form)
For cylindrical equation put \(x=ρ cos⁡ϕ\)
, \(y=sinϕ\) and \( z=z \)
⸫ \(ρ^2 cos^2⁡ϕ+ρ^2 sin^2⁡ϕ+ z^2=4^2\)
⸫\(ρ^2+z^2=16\) …(is cylindrical form)
